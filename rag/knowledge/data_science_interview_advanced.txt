Title: Advanced Data Scientist Interview Preparation
Intent: interview_prep
Role: data scientist
Specialization: experimentation, causal inference, offline metrics, model diagnostics
Seniority: 1-3 years
Tags: ab testing, causal inference, feature leakage, model calibration, business impact

Advanced Data Scientist Interview Blueprint

Round Types:
1. Case-study problem framing
2. Experiment design and statistical reasoning
3. Model diagnostics and metric trade-offs
4. Product impact storytelling
5. Behavioral execution and stakeholder alignment

Core Topics:
- A/B testing design: hypothesis, randomization unit, sample size, guardrail metrics
- Causal inference methods: propensity scores, difference-in-differences, and confounding control
- Offline metrics versus online metrics and when they diverge
- Feature leakage identification and prevention in production pipelines
- Precision, recall, calibration, and threshold optimization for business outcomes

Question Bank:
- Design an experiment to measure retention impact of a recommendation feature.
- How would you choose between precision and recall for fraud or risk detection?
- Explain how offline metrics can mislead product decisions.
- How do you detect and handle causal confounders in observational data?
- Describe a case where model calibration changed launch decisions.

Evaluation Rubric:
- Statistical correctness under uncertainty
- Causal reasoning quality and assumptions clarity
- Metric selection aligned to business objective
- Ability to explain trade-offs to non-technical stakeholders
- Decision quality with incomplete data

30-60-90 Day Preparation Plan:
30 days:
- Practice case-study decompositions and hypothesis trees.
- Revisit statistics and experiment power calculations.

60 days:
- Solve end-to-end A/B testing exercises with metric interpretation.
- Build causal inference mini-projects with transparent assumptions.

90 days:
- Create a mock decision memo comparing model and experiment outcomes.
- Conduct peer mock interviews focused on experimentation and communication.

Common Mistakes:
- Optimizing a single metric without guardrails
- Ignoring heterogeneous treatment effects
- Reporting correlation as causation
- Skipping uncertainty intervals in recommendations

Portfolio Signals:
- Experiment analysis notebooks with clear statistical reasoning
- Causal inference case study with reproducible pipeline
- Decision memos that connect model metrics to business impact
